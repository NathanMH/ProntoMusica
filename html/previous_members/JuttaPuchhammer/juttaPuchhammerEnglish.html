JUTTA PUCHHAMMER

    The Viennese-born violist Jutta Puchhammer-Sédillot , described as a violist of exceptional qualitity, is in great demand on the Canadian and international music scene.

    Ms. Puchhammer-Sédillot graduated from the Wiener Hochschule für Musik und Darstellende Kunst under Siegfried Führlinger with honors and holds a masters degree from the Eastman School of Music in Rochester, New York under Heidi Castleman.

    She is currently full time professor of viola and chamber music at the University of Montréal and is on the faculties of the Orford Arts Center (Quebec,Canada), the North American Viola Institute (NAVI) and the new TTT (teach teachers teach) Project in Villaricca,Chile.and has taught at the Perlman Music Programm (PMP).

    Her regular masterclass activities bring her to Schools such as the Juilliard School, Sony University, the Conservatoire supérieure de Lyon and the CRR in Paris, the Univerrsity of Vienna, and the University national de Bogota, Columbia and the Canadian Universities.

    During her career she has been founding member of the Wiener Nonett, Claudel Stringquartett, the Kegelstadt Trio, the String Trio of Montreal , the Ondine Piano Quartett, the Ensemble La Pieta as well as the Puchhammer-Desjardins Duo touring all over the globe. She is actually principal viola of the Laval Symphony orchestra with whom she premiered in 2013 the viola concerto by Tim Brady for her, which she will present at the 42nd International Viola Congress in Porto in november 2014 and record it with the Nova Scotia Symphony in january 2015. Numerous solo engagements with orchestras in Vienna, Montreal, Laval and South America, as well with diverse chamber groups complete her busy artistic life.

    Juttas interest goes towards the discovery of lost, unknown pieces around the turn of the 20th century . Her latest research covers the « Pièces de Concours » written between 1896 and 1930 for the final juries at the conservatoire supérieure de Paris. She ris currently reediting many of these in 3 volumes for the Schott Edition, Germany, and will record them in the near future to complete her research.

    Her solo CDs, entitled “Alto Romantic Fantasies” (Eclectra ECCD-2060) with works by C. Reinecke, J.Joachim, J..Fuchs, Ph.Scharwenka, ,and German Romantic Works (Fidelio FIDC 018) with sonatas by E. Naumann, F. Kiel and J.Fuchs, have both been acclaimed in Fanfare magazine with praise

    She has been the president of the Canadian Viola Society (2006-2014). and is since januray 2014 vice-presidet of the International viola society. She has been awarded the Maurice Riley Award, Prize given by the IVS for international achievement.
