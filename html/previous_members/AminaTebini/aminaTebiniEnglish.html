AMINA TÉBINI

    Montreal violist Amina Myriam Tébini holds a Master of Music from the Yale School of Music where she studied viola with Ettore Causa and chamber music with the Tokyo String Quartet, with the support from the F. Wickes fellowship. She also received her Artist Diploma and her Bachelor of Music from McGill University, where she studied as a Schulich Scholar with André Roy, Brian Chen and Anna-Belle Marcotte.

    She performed often as principal and assistant with the Yale Philharmonia, McGill Contemporary Ensemble, Aldeburgh World Orchestra and the newly formed Novus NY chamber ensemble. Her recent orchestral performances brought her to different stages, such as the Montreal Jazz Fest, the Concertgebouw (Amsterdam), Royal Albert Hall (London), le Poisson Rouge (NYC) and the Isaac Stern auditorium at Carnegie Hall (NYC).

    Amina is currently teaching viola at CEGEP Vanier. An active musician from the Montreal scene, she most recently performed as a member of Epsilon, quintette à cordes, the OSM and the Orchestre symphonique de Québec.
