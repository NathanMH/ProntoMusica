ANAÏS VIGEANT

    Contrabassist Anaïs Vigeant graduated with a first prize with great distinction unanimously from the Montreal Conservatory of Music, continuing her studies at McGill University, where she completed an Artist's Diploma. Recipient of the Joseph McAbbie scholarship, she won the "Début" CBC prize, reserved for emerging soloists.

    Ms. Vigeant has performed on several European and Canadian tours. She participated in major concerts at the Montreal Jazz Festival with Pink Martini, George Benson and Chris Botti. She has also performed in master classes with Rainer Zepperitz of the Berlin Philharmonic, Werner Zeibig of the Staatskapelle Dresden, François Rabbath and Joel Quarrington.

    She makes regular appearances in various orchestras including the Orchestre Métropolitain du Grand Montréal, Orchestre Symphonique de Longueuil, Orchestre Symphonique de Sherbrooke, Orchestre Symphonique de Drummondville, Orchestre symphonique de Trois-Rivières, La Sinfonia de Lanaudière, Pronto Musica, L’Opéra théatre de Rimouski, l’Orchestre Lyrique de Montréal, l’Orchestre de chambre de Montréal et l’Orchestre Symphonique du Festival de Lanaudière. In 2011, she co-founded the Epsilon String Quintet. From 2010 to 2012 she was a member of the Société pour les arts en milieux de santé. She is now a professor of double bass at three high schools: Pierre-Laporte, Saint-Edmont and Saint-Luc.
